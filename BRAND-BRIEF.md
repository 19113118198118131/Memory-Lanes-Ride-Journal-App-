# Memory Lanes — Logo & Brand Mark Brief

*A brief for a freelance illustrator/logo designer working in the vintage-badge tradition.*

## The product in one line
Memory Lanes is a motorcycle ride journal: riders upload their GPS ride, replay it on a map, get technique coaching, and keep a personal journal of their best rides.

## What we need
A single primary **badge-style brand mark**, delivered as vector (SVG + AI/EPS), plus the variants listed at the end. This is the emotional anchor of the whole product: it goes on the landing page, the login screen, the app icon, and — most importantly — stamped onto the ride cards riders export and share.

## Aesthetic direction
Heritage / vintage badge, in the tradition of American workwear, national-park insignia, and motorcycle-club patches. The clearest single reference is **Allan Peters (Peters Design Company)** — his Rush Creek, Heartland, and outdoor/badge work. Study that portfolio before starting.

Mechanics that define the look we want:
- **One bold idea, not a busy scene.** A single strong central motif that reads instantly at any size.
- **Circular or shield enclosure**, with the wordmark arcing along the top and/or bottom edge.
- **Thick, even line weights** — confident strokes that survive being shrunk to a 32px favicon. Nothing spindly or thin.
- **Geometric base with subtle hand-drawn warmth** — solid construction, but not cold or corporate.
- **Flat and print-first.** No gradients, no drop shadows, no 3D, no bevels. It should look like it could be screen-printed onto a leather jacket or embroidered on a patch.
- **Small accents** used sparingly: a star, a divider dot, a thin inner ring.

## Central motif ideas (designer's choice, these are starting points)
- A winding mountain road / switchback rendered as a clean ribbon or set of curves (the "lanes" of Memory Lanes).
- A stylized road disappearing toward a horizon or mountain.
- A route line that doubles as a heartbeat/pulse (the "memory" of a ride).
- A monogram "ML" integrated with a road curve.

Avoid: literal motorcycles, helmets, or engines (too on-the-nose and dates quickly), and anything that looks like a generic GPS-pin app icon.

## Wordmark
- Primary text: **MEMORY LANES**
- Optional secondary/tagline: **JOURNAL YOUR RIDE**
- A clean, slightly condensed sans or a subtle vintage sans fits the motorsport-meets-heritage tone. (The app UI uses *Rajdhani* for numerals; the logo type does not need to match, but shouldn't clash.)

## Colour
The app is deep-navy dark mode with a cyan/mint glow accent. The badge should work in that world:
- Primary brand colour: **cyan / mint** `#64ffda`
- Deep background: `#0a192f`
- Highlight / cream: an off-white for contrast within the badge
- **Must also work as a single-colour (one-ink) version** — solid cyan on dark, and solid dark on light.

## Deliverables
1. Primary full badge (colour, on dark) — SVG + AI/EPS + PNG
2. One-colour version (cyan-on-dark and dark-on-light)
3. Simplified icon-only mark (no wordmark) for the favicon / PWA app icon / small placements — must read at **32px and 192px**
4. Horizontal lockup (mark + wordmark side by side) for the site header
5. Clear-space and minimum-size guidance

## Where it will be used (so you can pressure-test legibility)
- Website header (~64px tall) and landing hero (large)
- Login screen (large, centred)
- Browser favicon (16–32px) and installed-app icon (192px, 512px)
- **Stamped onto exported ride-card images** (roughly 1080×1350 social format) — the icon-only or horizontal lockup will sit small in a corner, so corner-legibility matters

## Tone words
Premium, calm, crafted, heritage, trustworthy, a little adventurous. NOT: gamer, neon-arcade, techy-startup, cartoonish.
